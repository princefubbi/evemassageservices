Section 01

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small data files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. Cookies are widely used by website owners to make their websites work — or work more efficiently — as well as to provide reporting information.

Cookies set by the website owner (in this case, Eve Massage Services) are called first-party cookies. Cookies set by parties other than the website owner are called third-party cookies. Third-party cookies enable third-party features or functionality on or through the website — such as analytics. The parties that set these third-party cookies can recognise your device both when it visits our website and when it visits certain other websites.

In plain language A cookie is a small text file saved on your device. It helps websites remember you — for example, remembering your preferences, or counting how many people visited a page. Cookies cannot run programmes or deliver viruses to your device.

We do not use cookies to collect sensitive personal information such as health data, financial information, or government identity numbers.

Section 05

What Happens If You Disable Cookies?

You can choose to disable some or all cookies. Here is what that means for your experience on this website:

  • Strictly necessary cookies disabled: The website will still load and display correctly. Our website does not currently require strictly necessary cookies to deliver core functionality.
  • Analytics cookies disabled: You can still use the full website without any restrictions. Disabling analytics cookies simply means we will not receive data about your visit, and our ability to understand how the site is being used will be reduced.
  • All cookies disabled: The website will continue to function normally. However, if we add features in the future that rely on cookies (such as saved preferences or login sessions), those features may not work correctly.
Our position We do not gate content, services, or features behind cookie acceptance. You can read every page, view all services, and contact us via WhatsApp without accepting any cookies.

Section 07

Other Tracking Technologies

Cookies are not the only way to recognise visitors to a website. We may use other, similar technologies from time to time, including:

  • Web beacons (also called tracking pixels or clear gifs) — tiny graphics files that contain a unique identifier enabling us to recognise when someone has visited a page or opened an email. These are used to monitor traffic patterns and measure the success of communications.
  • Local storage — a browser feature that allows websites to store data locally on your device, similar to cookies but with larger capacity. We may use this for saving your preferences such as your last-viewed service type.

In most cases, these technologies work alongside cookies. Declining cookies in your browser settings will also impair the functioning of these technologies where they depend on cookies to operate.
