Signature Service

Deep Tissue Massage

Deep tissue massage applies firm, sustained pressure to the deeper layers of muscle and connective tissue. It is one of the most clinically studied forms of therapeutic massage, with documented effects on chronic pain, muscular tension, and circulatory function. Unlike relaxation massage, deep tissue targets specific anatomical structures — releasing adhesions, improving range of motion, and restoring normal tissue function.

60 / 90 / 120 min Moderate–Firm Pressure Home Service Available

What to expect

Your therapist will use slow, deliberate strokes and direct pressure across the grain of the muscle. You may feel mild discomfort in areas of tension — this is normal and subsides quickly. Most clients report significant relief within 24 hours of the session.

  • Reduces chronic muscle tension and postural pain accumulated from prolonged sitting or repetitive movement
  • Improves blood circulation, accelerating the delivery of oxygen and nutrients to fatigued tissue
  • Lowers cortisol levels measurably — reducing the physiological burden of chronic stress
  • Increases range of motion by breaking down scar tissue and releasing fascial restrictions
  • Clinically shown to reduce blood pressure in individuals with hypertension

Best for: Desk workers · Chronic back pain · Athletes · Stress-related tension · Post-injury recovery

60 / 90 / 120 min

Smooth basalt stones are heated and placed at key anatomical points across the body while the therapist uses others as an extension of their hands. Heat penetrates 3–4 centimetres into muscle tissue — significantly deeper than manual pressure alone — causing vasodilation, accelerated metabolic waste clearance, and profound muscular relaxation.

Lymphatic Drainage

60 / 90 min

A precise, rhythmic technique using light pressure to stimulate the lymphatic vessels just beneath the skin. Because the lymphatic system has no internal pump, it depends entirely on external stimulation to move lymph fluid. This treatment reduces oedema, supports immune function, and clears metabolic waste that accumulates in stagnant tissue — particularly after surgery, illness, or prolonged inactivity.

Best for: Post-surgical swelling · Frequent illness · Fluid retention · Detoxification · Immune support

Stroke Recovery Therapy

Neurological Rehabilitation Support

Stroke recovery is a complex, multi-phase process that extends well beyond acute medical care. Research consistently shows that structured physical therapy — including massage and targeted movement work — significantly improves functional outcomes in stroke survivors by stimulating neuroplasticity, restoring circulation to affected limbs, and reducing spasticity in paralysed or weakened muscle groups.

Our programme is designed to complement the patient's existing medical care and is coordinated with their clinical team where applicable.

  • Reduces spasticity and rigidity in stroke-affected limbs through targeted neuromuscular techniques
  • Stimulates peripheral circulation in areas of reduced motor function, supporting tissue health
  • Passive and assisted movement therapy to maintain joint range of motion during recovery
  • Supports psychological recovery — therapeutic touch has measurable effects on depression and isolation in stroke patients
  • Home visits available for patients with limited mobility across Akwa Ibom

Diabetes Support Therapy

Circulatory & Peripheral Health Programme

Diabetes affects the vascular and nervous systems progressively, leading to poor circulation, peripheral neuropathy, and delayed wound healing — particularly in the lower extremities. Therapeutic massage and complementary physical interventions have been shown in peer-reviewed research to improve insulin sensitivity, reduce neuropathic pain, and enhance blood flow to areas of compromised circulation.

This programme is not a replacement for medical treatment. It is a structured complementary therapy designed to improve quality of life and manage the physical complications of diabetes in coordination with the patient's physician.

  • Improves peripheral circulation in the feet and legs — the areas most vulnerable to diabetic complications
  • Reduces neuropathic pain and the sensation of numbness or tingling in the extremities
  • Clinical evidence suggests regular massage can improve HbA1c markers in some Type 2 diabetic patients
  • Foot care and monitoring incorporated into every session to detect early skin or nail changes
  • Stress reduction — lower cortisol directly supports more stable blood glucose regulation

FAQ

Common questions

Do I need to undress for a massage? +

You undress to your level of comfort. Most clients undress fully or to their underwear, but you are always fully draped with a sheet and only the area being worked on is exposed at any time. Your privacy and comfort are paramount.

What should I do before my session? +

Arrive well hydrated. Avoid eating a heavy meal within 1–2 hours of your session. If you are having a home service, prepare a quiet, warm space. Shower beforehand if possible. Remove jewellery and avoid applying heavy lotions or oils to your skin.

Can I combine two services in one visit? +

Yes. The most popular combination is the herbal steam bath followed by a deep tissue or aromatherapy massage. The steam opens the pores and relaxes the muscle tissue, significantly deepening the effectiveness of the massage that follows. Let us know at booking and we will schedule the time accordingly.

Is home service available for all treatments? +

All massage services are available as home service across Akwa Ibom. The herbal steam bath is currently available at our store only as it requires specialist equipment. A transport surcharge may apply depending on your location — this will be confirmed at booking.

Is massage safe if I have a medical condition? +

For most conditions, therapeutic massage is safe and beneficial. However, certain conditions require adaptation or medical clearance first — including active blood clots, severe osteoporosis, open wounds, fever, or recent surgery. Please disclose your full health history at booking and our team will advise appropriately.

How often should I book sessions? +

For general wellbeing and stress management, once or twice per month is sufficient. For chronic pain, athletic recovery, or clinical conditions, more frequent sessions — weekly or bi-weekly — yield significantly better outcomes. Our monthly packages are designed around the optimal treatment frequency for each duration.

What happens after a deep tissue session? +

Some clients experience mild soreness in treated areas for 24–48 hours — this is a normal response as the tissue heals and toxins are flushed out. Drink plenty of water after your session. Most clients feel significantly better by the following morning. A warm bath with Epsom salts can help if soreness is notable.

Do you offer sessions for elderly clients? +

Yes. We have significant experience working with elderly clients and adapt pressure, positioning, and technique accordingly. Gentle massage is particularly beneficial for older adults — improving circulation, reducing joint stiffness, and supporting mental wellbeing. Home service is often the most practical option for elderly clients and is fully available.
